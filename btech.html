Chapter One

And Just Like That… It's Over.

Four years.
Eight semesters.
Endless assignments, internals, records, projects, and sleepless nights.

Some days began with exhaustion. Some nights ended with tears. There were moments I questioned everything: "Why did I even choose this course?"

The fear of the future. The pressure of placements. The constant thought: "Am I really good enough?" It wasn't easy. It was one of the toughest phases of my life.

But college life at Sahrdaya wasn't a fairytale. It wasn't perfect. It wasn't always smooth. But it was real. Raw. Life-changing.

Taking the Leap

At the start, I didn't know what to expect. But I knew I didn't want to be "just a student" attending classes and chasing grades. So, I took a leap. I joined IEEE.

Not to fill my resume. Not to tick a box. But to push myself beyond my comfort zone. To discover who I could become.

Through IEEE, I found purpose, growth, friendships, and the thrill of creating something meaningful.

We bunked classes. We conducted events. We brainstormed ideas. We built memories that will last a lifetime.

To Sahrdaya…

You weren't always my favorite. I've complained about you more times than I can count. But looking back, I realize… You shaped me. You tested me. You frustrated me. And in the end - you transformed me.

To Everyone Who Made This Journey Special…

To the teachers who supported us not just in classrooms, but in labs, corridors, and staff rooms - listening to our complaints and calming our fears.

To the seniors who guided us, and the juniors who looked up to us - you were all part of this story.

To my parents - thank you for being my strength when I wanted to give up.

To my friends and loved ones - for seeing me at my lowest, celebrating my highs, and standing by me through it all.

And to God - for carrying me through every tough moment, for giving me strength when I felt weak.

This Is Not the End…

This isn't just a degree. It's every tear, every laugh, every fear, every late night, and every lesson. It's a tribute to everyone who was part of this journey.

Thank you, Sahrdaya.
Thank you, IEEE.
Thank you to everyone who made these four years unforgettable.

This is not the end. It's just the beginning. Miles to go.
